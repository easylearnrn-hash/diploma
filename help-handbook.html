Official Policy Document
Student Evaluation, Attendance,
Appeals, and Remediation Policy
Armenian College of Nursing & Health Sciences
Pre-Licensure Registered Nursing Program
Effective: Academic Year 2025 | Version 1.0
Section 1

Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to define the standards and procedures governing student evaluation, grading, attendance, appeals, and remediation in the Pre-Licensure Registered Nursing Program. These standards are established to ensure academic rigor, clinical safety, and compliance with nursing regulatory expectations.

Section 2

Grading Standards

2.1 Minimum Passing Grade

  • The minimum passing grade for all nursing courses is 75%.
  • A final grade below 75% constitutes course failure.
  • Grades are assigned using a numeric and letter-grade system approved by the institution.

2.2 Grading Scale

Percentage Letter Grade Status
90–100 A Excellent
80–89 B Satisfactory
75–79 C Minimum Passing
Below 75 F Failing
Section 3

Components of Evaluation

Final course grades are calculated using the following weighted components:

Component Weight
Examinations & Tests 40%
Assignments & Assessments 15%
Clinical, Skills & Simulation 25%
Attendance & Professionalism 20%
Total 100%
Section 4

Attendance Policy

4.1 Attendance Requirement

Attendance is mandatory for all:

  • Didactic classes
  • Skills laboratories
  • Simulation activities
  • Clinical rotations

4.2 Attendance Weight

Attendance and professionalism constitute 20% of the final course grade.

4.3 Excessive Absences

More than two (2) absences within a single calendar month will result in:

Immediate administrative removal (drop) from the course

Students dropped due to attendance violations are not eligible for:

  • Grade appeals
  • Remediation
  • Course completion

Attendance policies are strictly enforced due to patient safety and regulatory requirements.

Section 5

Clinical Performance Requirement

5.1 Clinical Safety Standard

Students must demonstrate safe and competent clinical practice at all times.

5.2 Clinical Failure

  • Failure to meet minimum clinical safety or competency standards results in course failure, regardless of the calculated numeric grade.
  • Clinical failure decisions are final and not subject to appeal.

Clinical safety overrides all other grade components. Patient safety is non-negotiable.

Section 6

Grade Calculation

Final grades are calculated using the following formula:

Official Grade Calculation Formula
Final Grade =
(Exams × 0.40) +
(Assignments × 0.15) +
(Clinical × 0.25) +
(Attendance × 0.20)

Final grades are rounded to one decimal place.

Section 7

Grade Appeals Policy

7.1 Grounds for Appeal

A student may submit a grade appeal only on the basis of:

  • Mathematical or calculation error
  • Recording or data entry error
  • Misapplication of an established policy

Disagreement with course difficulty, exam content, or instructor judgment does NOT constitute valid grounds for appeal.

7.2 Appeal Timeline

  • Appeals must be submitted within five (5) calendar days of grade posting.
  • Appeals submitted after this period will not be considered.

7.3 Appeal Review

  • Appeals are reviewed by the course instructor and program administration as appropriate.
  • Decisions are issued in writing and are final.
Section 8

Non-Appealable Decisions

The following actions are not subject to appeal:

  • Course removal due to attendance violations
  • Clinical safety failure
  • Final course grades below the minimum passing standard
  • Professional conduct or ethical violations

These decisions protect patient safety and program integrity. They are final and binding.

Section 9

Remediation Policy

9.1 Purpose of Remediation

Remediation is an academic support process designed to address identified learning deficiencies. Remediation does not replace earned grades.

9.2 Eligibility for Remediation

A student may be eligible for remediation if all of the following conditions are met:

  • Final course grade between 70% and 74.9%
  • Clinical performance deemed safe
  • Attendance requirements met
  • No professional conduct violations

9.3 Ineligibility for Remediation

Remediation is not available if:

  • Final grade is below 70%
  • Clinical safety standards were not met
  • Student was dropped due to attendance violations
  • Multiple course failures have occurred

9.4 Remediation Limits

  • Remediation may raise a failing grade only to the minimum passing grade of 75%
  • Remediation is permitted once per course
  • No letter grade higher than C may be awarded through remediation

Remediation is a privilege, not a right. It is offered at faculty discretion to support student success.

Section 10

Course Repetition & Progression

  • A nursing course may be repeated one time only
  • Failure of two or more nursing courses results in dismissal from the program
  • Re-entry is subject to academic review and institutional approval

Two nursing course failures = Automatic program dismissal

Section 11

Regulatory Alignment Statement

This policy is consistent with:

  • National Council of State Boards of Nursing (NCSBN) standards
  • U.S. Board of Nursing educational expectations
  • NCLEX-RN competency and clinical judgment requirements

The College reserves the right to enforce these policies to ensure academic integrity, public safety, and regulatory compliance.

Section 12

Policy Authority

This policy applies to all students enrolled in the Pre-Licensure Registered Nursing Program and is effective upon publication in the Student Handbook. The College reserves the right to amend this policy as required by regulatory or institutional needs.
