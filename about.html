About Us

Mission, Vision, and Values

Mission

The mission of the Armenian College of Nursing & Health Sciences is to educate and prepare competent, ethical, and practice-ready nurses who are equipped to provide safe, evidence-based, and compassionate care across diverse healthcare settings. The College is committed to delivering high-quality nursing education grounded in scientific knowledge, clinical excellence, and professional standards that support licensure eligibility and lifelong learning.

Vision

The Armenian College of Nursing & Health Sciences aspires to be a leading institution in nursing education, recognized for academic rigor, clinical excellence, and global relevance. The College seeks to advance the nursing profession by preparing graduates who demonstrate clinical competence, critical thinking, and professional integrity, and who are capable of contributing meaningfully to healthcare systems locally and internationally.

Values

The Armenian College of Nursing & Health Sciences is guided by the following core values:

Excellence

We are committed to high standards in teaching, learning, clinical practice, and academic integrity, ensuring graduates are prepared for safe and effective nursing practice.

Professionalism

We uphold ethical conduct, accountability, respect, and responsibility in all academic and clinical environments.

Patient Safety

We prioritize patient safety, quality of care, and evidence-based practice as foundational principles of nursing education.

Clinical Competence

We emphasize hands-on learning, clinical judgment, and skill development through simulation, supervised clinical practice, and real-world healthcare experiences.

Integrity

We promote honesty, transparency, and ethical decision-making in education, clinical practice, and professional relationships.

Lifelong Learning

We foster intellectual curiosity, critical thinking, and continuous professional development to support evolving nursing practice and healthcare needs.

Global Perspective

We prepare graduates to practice nursing within diverse cultural, social, and healthcare contexts, supporting both local service and global mobility.
