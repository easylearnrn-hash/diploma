How Your Grades Are Calculated
At the Armenian College of Nursing & Health Sciences, your grades are designed to reflect knowledge, clinical skill, professionalism, and attendance ‚Äî all of which are essential for safe nursing practice and NCLEX success.

This article explains exactly how your final grade is calculated, what is required to pass, and what can cause you to be removed from a class.

Minimum Passing Standard

üìå 75% is the minimum passing grade for all nursing courses.

  • Any final grade below 75% is considered failing, regardless of performance in other areas.

This standard follows U.S. nursing education requirements and Board of Nursing expectations.

What Counts Toward Your Grade

Your final course grade is made up of four parts:

1. Exams & Tests ‚Äî 40%

Includes:

  • System/unit exams
  • NCLEX-style questions
  • Case-based and NGN-style exams

These exams test your understanding of nursing concepts and clinical judgment.

2. Assignments & Assessments ‚Äî 15%

Includes:

  • Quizzes
  • Case studies
  • Care plans
  • Written or online assignments

These help reinforce learning and critical thinking.

3. Skills, Simulation & Clinical Performance ‚Äî 25%

Includes:

  • Skills lab check-offs
  • Simulation (OSCEs)
  • Clinical instructor evaluations

‚ö†Ô∏è Clinical safety is mandatory.
Failure to demonstrate safe clinical practice results in course failure, even if your numeric grade is passing.

4. Attendance & Professionalism ‚Äî 20%

Attendance is a core requirement of nursing education.

This portion reflects:

  • Class attendance
  • Punctuality
  • Professional behavior
  • Participation in required learning activities
Attendance Policy (VERY IMPORTANT)
  • Attendance is mandatory for all classes, labs, and clinical activities.
  • More than 2 absences in a single month will result in:

‚ùó Automatic removal (drop) from the course

This rule exists to protect patient safety and ensure adequate clinical preparation.

‚ö†Ô∏è There are no exceptions for excessive absences in nursing courses.

Automatic Course Failure Conditions

You will fail or be removed from a course if any of the following occur:

  • Final grade is below 75%
  • Clinical performance is unsafe
  • More than 2 absences in a month
  • Serious violations of professional conduct
Why These Rules Exist

Nursing education prepares students for real patient care, where:

  • Attendance is mandatory
  • Safety is non-negotiable
  • Accountability is essential

These policies align with U.S. Boards of Nursing and NCLEX expectations.
